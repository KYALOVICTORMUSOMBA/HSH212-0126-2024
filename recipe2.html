Nyama Choma

Kenya’s beloved grilled meat experience

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 1 hr
Serves: 4-5 people

About This Dish

Nyama Choma, meaning “roasted meat” in Swahili, is a cornerstone of Kenyan cuisine. This dish is as much a social event as it is a meal—often served during gatherings, celebrations, or simply on weekends with friends and family.

Traditionally made using goat meat, Nyama Choma can also be prepared with beef, chicken, or lamb. The meat is seasoned simply, then slow-grilled over an open flame for a smoky and charred flavor that is unmistakably Kenyan.

Pair it with kachumbari (a fresh tomato and onion salad) and ugali for an authentic experience. Whether you're hosting a barbecue or just craving some grilled perfection, this is the recipe for you.

Print Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Goat meat (bone-in preferred)
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp Fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp Lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 tsp Salt
  • 1 tsp Black pepper
  • 1 tsp Paprika (optional)
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable oil

Tip: Marinate the meat overnight for deeper flavor.

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Meat

    In a bowl, mix garlic, ginger, lemon juice, salt, pepper, paprika, and oil. Rub the mixture thoroughly into the meat. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for best results.

  2. Prepare the Grill

    Set up a charcoal grill for indirect heat. Let the coals become hot and white before placing the meat on the grill.

  3. Grill Slowly

    Place the meat on the grill. Cook slowly, turning occasionally, for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat is cooked through and has a nice char on the outside.

  4. Check for Doneness

    Use a meat thermometer (if available) or cut into the thickest part to ensure it's fully cooked but still juicy.

  5. Serve

    Slice and serve hot with kachumbari and ugali or your preferred sides. Enjoy the feast!
