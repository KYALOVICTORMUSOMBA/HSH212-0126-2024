Kenyan Pilau

A fragrant and flavorful rice dish

Prep Time: 20 mins
Cook Time: 45 mins
Serves: 4-6 people

About This Dish

Kenyan Pilau is a beloved dish across East Africa, particularly in Kenya. This fragrant rice dish is infused with aromatic spices and cooked with tender meat, creating a flavorful one-pot meal that's perfect for special occasions and family gatherings.

The rich blend of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves gives this dish its distinctive aroma and flavor. In Kenya, Pilau is traditionally served at weddings, during celebrations, or as a Sunday family meal.

This recipe has been passed down through generations in my family, and I'm excited to share it with you. The combination of tender beef, aromatic rice, and the perfect blend of spices makes this dish a true comfort food that will transport you to the heart of Kenya.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 500g beef, cubed
  • 1 large onion, finely sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon pilau masala*
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 beef stock cube (optional)
  • 3 cups water or beef broth
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish (optional)

*You can make pilau masala by grinding together: 1 tbsp cumin seeds, 1 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tsp black peppercorns, ½ tsp cardamom seeds, 4 cloves, and a small cinnamon stick.

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until golden brown and caramelized.
  3. Add garlic and ginger, cooking for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the pilau masala and cook for another minute to toast the spices.
  5. Add the beef cubes and brown on all sides until fully sealed.
  6. Mix in the chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and form a thick paste.
  7. Pour in the water or beef broth and bring to a boil.
  8. Add the rice and stir well. Taste the broth and adjust salt as needed.
  9. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.
  10. Turn off the heat and let the pilau sit covered for another 10 minutes to steam.
  11. Fluff gently with a fork and garnish with chopped coriander if desired.
  12. Serve hot with kachumbari or a side of plain yogurt.
